# Carbon Emissions Calculator for Companies (Scope 1, 2, 3)

## Overview

A corporate carbon emissions calculator for Scope 1, 2, and 3. Designed for easy sharing and demonstration of emissions estimation based on energy use, production, and financial data. It translates operational and financial data into **clear, standardized emissions insights**, enabling users to understand and benchmark their carbon footprint.

## What This Tool Does

- Estimates carbon emissions using multiple approaches:
  - Energy consumption  
  - Production output  
  - Revenue and operational metrics  
- Covers **Scope 1, Scope 2, and Scope 3 emissions**  
- Provides emissions breakdowns across sources and categories  
- Enables comparison across different estimation methodologies  

---

## Why This Matters

Corporate carbon data is often incomplete, inconsistent, and difficult to compare.  

This tool provides a **standardized and practical approach** to estimate emissions, helping organizations better understand their carbon footprint and support low-carbon decision-making.

---

## Methodology

### 1. Multi-Approach Emissions Estimation

The model applies different estimation methods depending on data availability:

- **Energy-based approach:** Converts energy consumption into emissions  
- **Production-based approach:** Estimates emissions based on production output levels  
- **Revenue-based approach:** Uses economic activity as a proxy for emissions  

---

### 2. Scope-Based Accounting Framework

According to the Greenhouse Gas Protocol (GHG Protocol), emissions are categorized as follows:

- **Scope 1:** Direct emissions from owned or controlled sources  
- **Scope 2:** Indirect emissions from purchased energy  
- **Scope 3:** Other indirect emissions across the value chain (there're 15 scope-3 categories in total)

---

### 3. Emissions Factor Application

The model uses **localized and sector-specific emissions factors** to translate activity data into emissions.

These factors are derived from:
- National statistical datasets  
- Industry standards and guidelines  
- Carbon market benchmarks  

All factors are standardized to ensure consistency and comparability.

---

### 4. Data Inputs

Users provide company-level inputs such as:

- Energy consumption  
- Production output  
- Revenue and operational data  

These inputs are mapped to corresponding emissions sources within the model.

---

### 5. Results & Visualization

The tool generates:

- Total emissions estimates  
- Emissions breakdowns by scope and category  
- Comparative results across estimation methods  

All results are visualized through an interactive dashboard.
